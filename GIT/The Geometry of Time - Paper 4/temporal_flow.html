Temporal Flow — Coupled Shape Topology

How subsystems' temporal shapes feed into each other — the Van Gogh view

Each subsystem traces its own temporal shape. But systems don't exist in isolation — one subsystem's release feeds another's accumulation. The release arc of the storm lifecycle pours into the accumulation arc of the gust front. The gust front's release feeds the multicell pulse. The shapes flow into each other like currents in a painting — each swirl has its own centre, but the energy streams between them.

Reading the visualisation: Each swirl is a subsystem's temporal shape — blue arcs are accumulation (energy building), amber arcs are release (energy discharging). The flowing streams between shapes show coupling — energy flowing from one subsystem's release into another's accumulation. Thicker streams = stronger coupling. The particle flow shows the direction and rate of energy transfer. Red dashed streams = destructive feedback (the system fighting itself).

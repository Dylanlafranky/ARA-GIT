The Geometry of Time — Temporal Shapes

What does a system's accumulation-release cycle look like as geometry?

Every cyclical system traces a shape through time. During accumulation, energy builds — the radius grows outward. During release, energy discharges — the radius contracts. The ARA ratio determines how the cycle's angular territory is split between these phases, and therefore the shape the system draws.

360° ÷ (1 + φ) = 137.5°
The accumulation angle for a phi-ratio system is the golden angle — the exact angle sunflowers use to pack seeds without overlap.
The phyllotaxis connection isn't an analogy. It's literally the same geometry — one in space, one in time.

2 — Extruded Through Time: The Helix

Stack multiple cycles along a time axis and the 2D shape becomes a 3D structure. A phi system produces a helix where successive cycles never overlap — the golden angle ensures each new cycle's peak lands in the largest gap left by previous cycles. A resonant system (ARA = 2.0) overlaps every other cycle. A snap barely traces any arc at all.

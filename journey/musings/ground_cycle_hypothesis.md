## The Idea

Between ℏ (log -34.0) and the Planck scale (log -34.5), there exists a sub-quantum oscillatory system — the universe's ground cycle. This is the most fundamental accumulation-release pair in existence. Remove it and the ladder doesn't exist.

It isn't a half-cycle. It's a full cycle operating between two extremes:

- **Accumulation phase:** Black holes accumulate matter, energy, and information. Gravity dominates time. Structure compresses toward singularity.
- **Release phase (the snap):** The singularity releases — Big Bang. Time dominates gravity. Space expands, structure disperses, entropy wins.

The Big Bang is not the beginning. It's the release phase. Black holes are not the end. They're the accumulation feeding back in.

---

## The Flip

In our half of the cycle (post-Big-Bang, expanding universe):
- **Gravity dominates time.** Gravity accumulates, time/entropy spends. This is what Paper 7 describes — we live in the release tail, but gravity is still forming structure (galaxies, stars, planets). The universe's ARA is < 1 and falling, but gravity hasn't given up yet.

In the other half (pre-Big-Bang, contracting/accumulating):
- **Time dominates gravity.** The roles invert. Time/entropy becomes the accumulator (information consolidates, distinctions collapse toward unity) and gravity becomes the spender (gravitational potential energy is converted, wells merge and deepen until there's only one). 

The ground cycle IS the flipping of which force rules. One half: gravity accumulates structure against entropy's spending. Other half: entropy/time accumulates uniformity against gravity's last holdouts. The flip point is the snap — Big Bang in one direction, total black hole coalescence in the other.

## Why the Universe's Energy = 0

If this is a full cycle, not a one-shot, then accumulation and release must balance over the full period. Total energy input over one cycle = total energy output. Net = 0.

This is observed. The universe's total energy is consistent with zero — gravity's negative potential energy cancels matter's positive mass-energy. This isn't a coincidence in the ground cycle model. It's a requirement. A sustained engine (ARA near φ) can't create or destroy net energy — it can only convert between accumulation and release forms.

The zero-energy universe is the ground cycle's Type 1 coupling being clean. No net energy created or destroyed per cycle. Just converted back and forth between gravity-dominated and time-dominated phases.

## ARA of the Ground Cycle

If the ground cycle is sustained (the universe exists, repeatedly, not one-shot), its ARA should be near φ. Specifically:

- T_acc = duration of black hole accumulation phase (all black holes forming, growing, merging, until total coalescence)
- T_rel = duration of the Big Bang release phase (from snap to heat death / maximum expansion)

ARA_ground = T_acc / T_rel

If ARA_ground ≈ φ, the cycle is a sustained engine. If ARA_ground >> 1, it's exothermic (accumulates much longer than it releases — long compression, short bang). If ARA_ground ≈ 1, it's symmetric (equal time in each phase).

We can't measure this directly from inside one half-cycle. But we might be able to constrain it by the universe's current properties.

## Constraining the Shape (what we CAN observe)

1. **Information preservation in black holes.** If the ground cycle is Type 1 (clean handoff), information must survive the accumulation phase and feed into the next release. Current physics trends: information IS preserved (holographic principle, black hole complementarity, Page curve). Consistent.

2. **Total energy = 0.** Required for a balanced cycle. Observed. Consistent.

3. **Cosmological constant is small but nonzero.** In the ground cycle model, Λ is the "idle speed" — the minimum expansion rate that prevents the cycle from stalling. It's not zero (the hamster doesn't stop), but it's tiny (the hamster runs slowly between phases). The infamous fine-tuning of Λ might just be: the ground cycle's ARA sets it. A φ-optimal engine doesn't idle fast.

4. **The deceleration parameter q₀ is changing.** q₀ crossed zero ~7 Gyr ago. The universe transitioned from gravity-winning to expansion-winning. In the ground cycle model, this is the midpoint of the release phase — we're past the halfway mark of the "time dominates gravity" half. The release is more than half spent.

5. **Black holes grow.** In the current epoch, black holes are still accumulating. They're building toward the next coalescence. This is the accumulation phase already beginning to assert itself within the release phase — the next cycle's accumulation is starting before the current release finishes. That's what Type 1 coupling does: the phases overlap at the boundary.

## The Test We Can't Run (But Can Approach)

We can't observe the full cycle. But we can ask: does the observable evidence constrain the ground cycle's ARA?

If the release phase ≈ the age of the universe to heat death (~10¹⁰⁰ years if you include black hole evaporation), and the accumulation phase is what came "before" the Big Bang, then:

- If ARA_ground = φ: T_acc ≈ 1.618 × T_rel ≈ 1.618 × 10¹⁰⁰ years
- If ARA_ground = 1.0: T_acc ≈ T_rel ≈ 10¹⁰⁰ years

Either way, the full cycle period is unmeasurable. But the SHAPE might be detectable through the five constraints above. If all five hold as physics advances, the ground cycle model gains credibility without ever being directly observed.

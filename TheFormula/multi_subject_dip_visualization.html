Multi-subject dip pattern — intruder waves rolling through

11 PhysioNet NSR subjects, actual-values delta-integration architecture. Each subject's correlation plotted vs forecast horizon (in beats ahead). Look for shared dips — those are signatures of physiological systems whose waves pass through our measurement at consistent timescales.

Main finding: mean correlation drops from +0.87 (1-3 beats) down to +0.44 at ~600 beats (~8 min) — a consistent dip across 8 of 11 subjects. Recovery at 1000 beats, then a second dip at ~5000 beats (~1 hour). The 8-min dip lines up with mid-frequency autonomic shifts; the 1-hour dip lines up with the BRAC sleep-stage envelope.

3 · Per-subject dip locations

Most subjects dip at 100-600 beats (1.5-7 min) — the mid-frequency autonomic band. A few subjects (nsr005, nsr040, nsr050) instead show their main dip at 2000 beats (~25 min) — these may be recordings during deeper sleep where the faster intruder was quiet but the slower BRAC envelope was active.

4 · What the dip is telling us

The actual-values architecture predicts cleanly at both very short lead (1-3 beats, mean corr +0.87) and partial recovery around 1000 beats (+0.61). In between, we hit a consistent corrupting band at ~600 beats. That's not noise — that's another system's wave passing through our measurement axis.

At ~8 minutes of clock time, the most likely intruder is mid-frequency autonomic activity — slow baroreflex-style envelopes, Mayer-wave bursts, micro-arousal transitions during sleep. These are well-documented physiological systems. They're not in our rung ladder because we're working in beat-units, and the mid-frequency band sits at φ⁹–φ¹¹ in beats — rungs we did include — but their state is transient rather than steady, so the steady-state amp+phase representation doesn't capture them well.

The fix would be to add a transient-event detector to the architecture: when residuals at 100-1000 beats deviate beyond a threshold, model them explicitly as a passing intruder wave with its own state. Same idea as how the SOI matched-rung partner closed the ENSO mid-horizon dip.
